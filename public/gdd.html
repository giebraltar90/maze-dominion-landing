Vision & Identity

Maze Dominion is the standalone evolution of Warcraft 3's Line Tower Wars — taking the genre's greatest competitive loop and adding one mechanic that changes everything: units attack and destroy whatever blocks their path. No anti-cheat NPC. No arbitrary rules.

Defense and offense are inseparable.
Every wall you build is a decision.
Every unit you send is an attack and an investment.
The maze is not a barrier — it is a killing machine your opponent is trying to dismantle.

Design Pillars

Maze = Weapon
Pillar I

Every wall placement is a spatial decision with economic consequences. A well-placed Wood wall in front of a Cannon tower is worth more than a third Cannon tower.

Gold Is Never Safe
Pillar II

Three competing uses for every gold piece: rebuild breaches, send units, build new towers. This three-way tension is irreducible and must be preserved in all balance decisions.

No Passive Play
Pillar III

Not sending falls behind economically. Not repairing loses the maze. Every strategy requires active engagement every round. Turtling is a path to slow defeat.

Readable in 3 Seconds
Pillar IV

Any spectator must understand what's happening instantly. Breach events are visually loud. Tempo bar communicates advantage. Designed for streaming from the ground up.

Skill Always Wins
Pillar V

Comeback mechanics exist but never override superior play. The better player wins 80%+ of matches at equal mechanical level. No hard rubber-banding.

Infinite Replayability
Pillar VI

Named combos, seasonal maps, maze sharing, hero draft, new unit unlocks per round. Every match plays differently. Community generates the meta.

WC3 Heritage & What We Solved

Maze Dominion is built on the shoulders of Line Tower Wars, Wintermaul Wars, and Teamline Tower Wars — the greatest competitive tower defense maps ever made. We inherit their soul and fix every flaw.

What WC3 Tower Wars Got Perfectly Right

  • Send units = permanent income. Every send is an investment, not just an attack. This creates the core economic tension that makes the genre strategic. We keep this exactly.
  • Unit counters built around tower weaknesses. Flying units ignore mazes. Attacking units destroy towers. Each unit type has a clear counter requiring specific tower combinations. We extend this.
  • Maze building as primary defense. Longer paths = more damage. Tight turns = more attacks per unit. Geometry is a skill. We add walls as a separate system on top.
  • Technology trees defining playstyle. Choosing Fire vs Ice vs Lightning changes how your entire maze functions. We expand this to 6 trees with exclusive T3 nodes.
  • Life stealing as win condition. Units that exit steal a life and give one to the sender. Creates visceral stakes every combat round. We keep this exactly.
  • Unit upgrade tiers during match. WC3 had tiered sends that unlocked per round. We formalize this with 3 explicit upgrade tiers per unit type. (Added to Maze Dominion.)
WC3 Expert Meta — Core Truth

"The goal is to make your opponent build slightly more than they had to, lowering their income relative to yours. The instant that happens, you win in a pro match."

This principle is the backbone of Maze Dominion's economy design. Everything is balanced around it.

Grid & Maze Architecture

Each player owns a 20×40 tile grid. Entry top-center (2 tiles wide). Exit bottom-center (2 tiles wide). The entire strategic game lives in this space — every tile decision is permanent until you repair or rebuild it.

Maze Archetypes

The Spiral
Max path length · Fast units shortcut it

Units wind inward then back out. Maximum tower exposure time. Vulnerable to Runners who breach the center and shortcut. Counter: Iron wall at center + Sniper towers.

The Funnel
Choke-point focus · Breach-bait heaven

Wide top narrows to 1-tile choke. Wood walls create deliberate breach bait — units stop while Cannons splash. High density = maximum splash efficiency.

The Gauntlet
Anti-tank · Iron walls required

Long parallel corridors with Iron walls. Forces Siege to commit fully. Extremely expensive. Countered by Runner swarms finding gaps.

The Web (Adaptive)
High skill · Multiple valid paths

Multiple paths through different tower types. Adapts to what opponent sends. Hardest to build, highest tournament representation.

The Breach Mechanic

Units don't reroute around obstacles. They attack and destroy whatever is in the way, then walk through the gap. The gap stays open until manually rebuilt. This replaces the WC3 anti-cheat NPC completely.

Complete Breach Algorithm

OnUnitBlocked(unit, blockedTile):
  1. Run A* treating ALL tiles as passable
  2. Find FIRST blocking tile on that path
  3. Check unit.targetType:
     WALL_FIRST → prefer walls
     TOWER_FIRST → prefer towers
     NEAREST → closest blocker
     NONE → reroute only (Runner)
  4. Attack target: HP -= unit.damage every attackInterval
  5. On HP = 0: remove tile, recalculate A*, resume
  6. Gap persists until Build Phase manual repair

Breach Baiting — The Core Tactic

Setup: Wood wall (150 HP, 5g) blocking path. 3 Cannon towers in 2-tile radius.

Result: Swarm stops to breach Wood wall. 3 Cannons fire splash AoE at stationary cluster. All 5 Swarm units die before wall breaks. Cost: 3g to repair wall. Opponent spent 30g. Net economic victory.

Counter: Send Siege instead. Siege ignores walls, targets Cannons directly (400 dmg/hit vs 450 HP). Two hits = Cannon destroyed. Wood bait worthless, towers gone.

Counter-counter: Frost Tower slows Siege approach. Warlord hero Snipe (instant delete). Stun Pylon freezes Siege mid-breach. This loop is the heart of the game.

Repair Rules
Walls repaired manually during Build Phase ONLY. No mid-combat repairs. Repair cost = 50% original. Damage persists between rounds until repaired. Choosing which breaches to repair vs abandon vs accept is a core skill test every Build Phase. Wall damage shows visual cracks at 75%, 50%, 25% HP thresholds.

Unit Upgrade Tiers — WC3 DNA Formalized

Like WC3 Tower Wars, every unit can be upgraded to stronger tiers before sending. Higher tiers cost more gold but unlock dramatically better combat stats, new behaviors, and bonus income on send. This creates a second layer of economic decisions every Send Phase.

How Unit Upgrades Work
In the Send Phase, select any unit type. Click "Upgrade" once (Tier 2) or twice (Tier 3) before queueing the send. Cost is additive — you pay the base cost plus upgrade cost. Higher tiers are locked until the round milestone is reached. You can still send T1 units after T2/T3 unlock — sometimes a cheap T1 Runner is the right call over an expensive T3.

The Overbuilding Trap

If opponent sends 30g of Runners and you spend 80g adding towers that weren't needed — you are 50g behind in net economy. Expert play: identify minimum required response to each send. Spend exactly that. Bank the rest for income sends. This compounds dramatically over 20 rounds.

Tempo Bar

Visible HUD bar in screen center (spectator-facing) + small corner version per player. Calculated every round. Cosmetic only — no mechanical effect.

Tempo Score = (My Income Rate - Opp Income Rate)
            + (My Lives - Opp Lives) × 2
            + (Towers I Destroyed - Towers Opp Destroyed) × 3
Positive → bar fills toward opponent (winning)
Negative → bar fills toward you (behind)

Round Structure — Full Specification

Phase 1 · 30 Seconds
Build Phase

Place walls, towers, repair breaches (50% cost), purchase research, reposition hero. Path validation every placement — red flash if sealing exit. You can sell towers at 60% value. Cannot repair mid-combat under any circumstances.

Key Decision Every Round
Repair last breach (defensive) vs build new tower (offensive) vs save for send (income). You rarely have gold for all three. This three-way tension is the game.
Phase 2 · 15 Seconds
Send Phase

Select units. Choose tier (T1/T2/T3). Apply traits. Time your sends strategically — Siege after opponent spends gold rebuilding. Bluff: queue units, cancel last second, bait over-building. In 2v2/4v4: coordinate sends with partner via ping system.

Phase 3 · Variable 10–120s
Combat Phase

All queued units spawn. Pathfind, breach, fight toward exit. Heroes activate abilities. Towers fire. No actions except hero abilities on CD. Phase ends when all units dead or exited. No sends = 0 seconds. Titan + full send = up to 90s.

Phase 4 · 3 Seconds
Income Phase

Gold ticks (15 + income rate). Lives updated. Round counter increments. Escalation unlocks checked. Tempo bar updates. Win condition checked. New round begins immediately.

Hero System — Complete Specification

Heroes are physical commanders who live inside your maze. They cannot block paths, can be attacked by units, level up during the match, and swing critical moments with high-impact abilities. The hero you pick defines your playstyle.

Universal Hero Rules
One hero per player, selected at match start. Heroes occupy one tile — engine enforces they cannot block valid maze path. Heroes can be targeted and damaged by enemy units within 2-tile aggro range. Hero death: 30s respawn at base position, all abilities on CD. Abilities 1/2/3 upgrade at levels 4/7/10. Ultimate unlocks at level 5.

4v4 — Lane Conquest (Flagship Mode)

Four players per team. Four parallel lanes. Three escalating phases. Dominating your lane makes you a Conqueror who invades remaining lanes. The match becomes organized chaos earned through individual excellence.

Phase 1 · Isolation
1v1 in each lane

All 4 lanes fully isolated. No cross-lane interaction. Individual life pools (20 each). Fight your mirrored opponent only. Winner of their lane first becomes a Conqueror.

Phase 2 · Invasion
On First Lane Defeat

Defeated player becomes spectator. Winner becomes Conqueror. May invade adjacent enemy lane (L or R neighbor only). 2-round delay before first invasion send. Income contribution: 60%. Hero abilities: 50% power in invaded lane. Invaded player receives +5g/round emergency bonus.

Phase 3 · Collapse
Multiple Defeats

2 enemy lanes defeated: Conquerors can target any remaining lane. 3 defeated: Team life pool activates (40 shared), hero abilities at full power. All 4 defeated: Victory. Phase 3 is the spectacle — dramatic, fast, stream-worthy.

Draft Mode — Asymmetric Starting Conditions

NEW · Not in WC3

Available in ranked 1v1 and organized tournaments from Season 2. Adds metagame depth, tournament drama, and endless strategic content.

Draft Format

Before match start, a 3-step draft phase runs: (1) Tower Ban: each player bans 2 tower types from the full roster — those towers are unavailable to BOTH players this match. (2) Hero Pick: alternating single pick. Player with lower ELO picks first. (3) Research Lock: each player secretly commits to 2 research trees before Round 1 (visible to opponent at Round 1 start). Research lock is advisory only — breaking it costs +20% research prices.

Tower bans create entirely different metas — ban Cannon and the game becomes about Chain + Inferno defense. Ban Frost and units move fast through everything. Ban Chain and Siege becomes unstoppable. Each combination is a completely different strategic puzzle.

Asymmetric Starting Condition (Optional Ranked Mode)

In a variant of ranked play (separate queue), players start with different conditions based on their last 5 match history: the player who recently lost more gets +15g starting gold and a free T1 tower of their choice. The player who won more starts with a +5g/round income bonus. Creates natural asymmetry making comeback matches mathematically fairer without compromising competitive integrity.

Why Draft Mode Is a Content Engine
  • Every ban combination = unique meta episode (streams, YouTube essays)
  • Pro players become known for signature ban patterns
  • Community discovers: "If you ban Frost + Cannon, Napalm Maze becomes unbeatable"
  • Patch notes now affect draft meta — content creators analyze weekly

Innovation Systems NEW — Not in WC3

These systems are what transform Maze Dominion from a great game into a game with a metagame, a community, and a reason to come back every week. Every single one of these is something WC3 Tower Wars never had.

Match Replay & Ghost System
NEW — Solves WC3's No Learning Tool Problem

After every match — win or lose — the player enters a post-match Review screen. This shows their maze overlaid with a "ghost replay" of the entire match.

What the Ghost System Shows
  • Breach heat map: Red-colored tiles show where walls/towers were breached, intensity of red = how many times breached. Instantly shows structural weaknesses.
  • Unit path overlay: Semi-transparent unit silhouettes show the exact paths all units took through your maze. Reveals shortcut routes you didn't know existed.
  • Gold efficiency per round: Mini timeline shows each round's gold spend vs gold earned. Highlights overbuilding rounds (red) and efficient rounds (green).
  • Leak analysis: For every unit that exited your maze, shows which breach point they used and how many lives it cost you.
  • Opponent comparison: Side-by-side view of both mazes. See exactly why their towers killed more efficiently than yours.
Why This Is Critical

WC3 Tower Wars players couldn't understand why they lost. No improvement loop beyond intuition. The Ghost System makes the learning curve visible. New players see "I was breached here 7 times — I need an Iron wall here." Intermediate players see "I spent 80g on Round 6 when 40g would have held." Expert players see "My opponent sent Siege specifically when my Gold Shrine was exposed."

This is also the single best content creation tool in the game. Streamers show ghost replays of their best and worst moments. YouTube essay content: "The most embarrassing breach in Maze Dominion history." Every replay is shareable via 6-digit code.

Named Combo Discovery System
NEW — Community Ownership Mechanic

When a player first triggers a new tower synergy pattern that results in an exceptionally efficient kill sequence, the ComboDatabase records it permanently with their name attached.

How It Works

The system monitors: tower combinations that fire within 0.5s of each other on the same unit, unit kill rates above 150% of baseline efficiency, and breach bait patterns that result in 90%+ unit kills before exit. When a new pattern is first detected: flash "NEW COMBO DISCOVERED" notification. Player types a name. It's permanently logged to the Metagame Hub as "The Coffin — discovered by [PlayerName], Round 5, Season 1."

Combos persist across patches unless they become impossible due to balance changes (archived with "(nerfed)" tag). Players build reputations around their discoveries. Top combo discoverers have a Hall of Fame page.

Why Players Love This
  • Your name is in the game forever — permanent legacy
  • Creates constant discovery motivation — "what hasn't been found yet?"
  • Content creators race to find undiscovered combos first
  • Metagame Hub becomes a wiki that players contribute to organically
  • Seasonal map changes create new combo possibilities each season
Intel & Scout System
NEW — Solves WC3's Full Visibility Problem

By default, you cannot see your opponent's maze layout. You know units are coming in. You see what types exit. But their build choices, tower positions, and maze geometry are hidden until scouted.

Scout Unit

Costs 10g. Purchased via Intel Research T1. Send Scout into opponent's maze — it reveals a 4×4 section for 20 seconds, then dies. Opponent receives "SCOUTED" notification (they know you've seen that section — creates a bluff layer). Scout units have 30 HP and are easily killed by towers — place them at entry and choose which section to target wisely.

Information Layers
  • No Intel research: See unit types spawning in real time. See leaked unit types. No maze visibility.
  • Intel T1 (Scouts): Scout units reveal 4×4 sections on demand. You choose where to look.
  • Intel T2 (Surveillance): See opponent's current gold at round end (±10g precision).
  • Intel T2 (Intercept): 5s preview of opponent's unit queue before their Send Phase ends.
  • Intel T3 (Dark Ops): YOUR sends are hidden from opponent for 3s post-spawn.
  • Intel T3 (Blackout): Opponent loses all vision for 6s — use with Titan send.
The Bluff Poker Layer

When you scout, your opponent knows. They may: rebuild their maze to hide the weakness you saw, send decoy units in a different direction, or use Phantom's Decoy Send ability to make you think Titan is coming while sending nothing. At high level, the decision to scout is itself strategic — sometimes you DON'T scout to let your opponent think you don't know what they're doing.

Tempo Bar System

Visible HUD bar spanning the top center of the screen. Also shown on spectator overlay in esports broadcast. Calculated every round end. Cosmetic only — no mechanical effect. Essential for streaming and casters.

Tempo = (My Income - Opp Income) × 1.0
      + (My Lives - Opp Lives) × 2.0  
      + (Towers I destroyed - towers opp destroyed) × 3.0
      + (Sends made this round - opp sends) × 1.5
Positive → bar fills toward opponent (you're winning)
Negative → bar fills toward you (you're behind)

Color coding: green when you're ahead, amber when close, red when behind. Subtle pulsing animation at extremes. Intentionally designed to create "will this comeback happen?" tension in spectator scenarios.

Maze Sharing System
NEW — Community Content Engine

After any match, save your current maze layout as a named template with a 6-digit share code. Load any shared maze as your starting layout for the next match.

How It Works

MazeSaveData serializes: tile positions (type + HP modifier), tower placements (type + upgrade level), hero starting position, and metadata (author, ELO, match record). Stored in Supabase with 6-digit alphanumeric code. Share code is viewable in post-match screen. Anyone with the code can load it. Community voting: upvote mazes that work well. Top-rated mazes featured on Metagame Hub weekly.

Maze of the Week

Every Wednesday, the top community-voted maze template is designated "Maze of the Week." A special challenge runs: use ONLY this starting maze template. Whoever achieves the best win rate with it earns a cosmetic badge. This is free content generation every week — the community does the design work, Maze Dominion provides the platform.

Seasonal Map Rotation
NEW — Prevents Meta Stagnation

Each season introduces 2–3 new grid template variants alongside the standard layout. Template variants change the terrain of the player grid — not the rules, but the starting geometry.

Why Seasonal Maps Matter

  • Creates seasonal meta resets — players must re-learn
  • Content creators analyze "best maze for The Narrows" every season

Balance Philosophy

Balance is the hardest part of competitive game design. The economy, breach mechanic, unit tier system, and hero abilities create emergent complexity that cannot be fully anticipated in advance.

No Dead Strategies

Every tower, unit tier, hero, and research tree must be viable in some meta context. If something is never built in high-level play, it needs buffing or its role needs clarifying. "Situationally useful" is valid. "Never useful" is not.

Counters Not Dominance

No unit should be "always send." No tower should be "always build first." Every strong option must have a clear, accessible counter. Strength should create counterplay options, not helplessness.

Economy Before Combat

A unit dealing 500 damage costing 200g generating +1/round income is balanced against a unit dealing 50 damage costing 25g generating +1/round income via economic value per gold, not raw combat stats alone.

WC3 Snowball Problem — Our Complete Solution

What WC3 Never Fixed

Falling behind on income at Round 5 in WC3 Tower Wars was effectively a death sentence. Players dropped out mid-match constantly. This killed game retention and player satisfaction. Maze Dominion has four separate comeback paths:

  • Deficit Bonus: +8g/round when below 5 lives. Mild — never overcomes a skill gap, but provides an economic floor.
  • Broker Hero: Specifically designed as a comeback tool. Emergency Funds (70g instant), Sabotage Income (opponent -40% tick), Black Market (any unit at 50% cost).
  • Siege as Economic Reset: A player far behind can send T2 Destroyer or T3 Annihilator. On success, destroys 200–500g of towers, temporarily erasing an economic lead.
  • Research Counter-Picks: Behind on Destruction research? Invest in Fortification Quick Repair — 100g makes their tower-damage sends recoverable quickly.

Comebacks are hard. They should be — skill matters. But they are always mathematically possible, and that makes every game worth finishing.

Balance Pipeline

  • Threshold: Any unit tier, tower, or hero appearing in 70%+ of winning matches over 1,000 games gets examined.
  • Pick rate vs win rate: Low pick + high win = hidden OP (buff visibility or direct nerf). High pick + low win = trap (buff or clarify role).
  • Unit tier balance: Each tier's gold efficiency (lives stolen or tower damage per gold spent) should be within 15% of comparable tier units at same unlock round.
  • Claude API balance loop: After each beta session, feed match log JSON: "Identify top 3 balance concerns, suggest adjustment ranges with reasoning." Weekly human review of AI suggestions.
  • Patch cadence: Every 6 weeks during active seasons. Max 5 balance changes per non-emergency patch. All changes with public reasoning. No silent nerfs.
